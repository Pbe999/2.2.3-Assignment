The Polar Exhibit

Stand on top of the world at 90° North. Experience one of the world's most powerful nuclear icebreakers, 50 Years of Victory. Get chances to see polar bears, walrus and other Arctic animals.

The Polar Bear

Polar bears are stocky, with a long neck, relatively small head, short, rounded ears, and a short tail. The male, which is much larger than the female, weighs 410 to 720 kg (900 to 1,600 pounds). It grows to about 1.6 metres (5.3 feet) tall at the shoulder and 2.2–2.5 metres in length.

The Penguin

Its body is fusiform (tapered at both ends) and streamlined. A penguin has a large head, short neck, and elongated body. The tail is short, stiff, and wedge-shaped. The legs and webbed feet are set far back on the body, which gives penguins their upright posture on land.
